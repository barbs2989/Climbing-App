## OVERVIEW & GEOGRAPHY

The **Enchantment Lakes Basin** is a premier alpine playground in the North Cascades, centered in the Alpine Lakes Wilderness ~15 miles southwest of Leavenworth, Washington. The basin comprises numerous interconnected alpine lakes and granite peaks ranging from 6,000 to 8,500 feet. The area is divided into five permit zones: **Snow Zone, Colchuck Zone, Core Zone, Stuart Zone,** and **Eightmile/Caroline Zone**, with the Core Zone being the highest-demand, most scenic section.

**Key Statistics:**
- **Primary Trailhead:** Snow Lakes (47.5441°N, -120.7097°W)
- **Distance from Leavenworth:** 4.2 miles via Icicle Creek Road
- **Primary Approach Distance:** 7 miles to Snow Lakes; additional 3+ miles to Core Zone
- **Elevation Range:** 1,300 ft (trailhead) to 8,520 ft (Enchantment Peak)
- **Access Season:** May 15–Oct 31 (overnight permits); day-use year-round
- **Permit System:** Highly competitive lottery (Feb 15–Mar 1 for 75% of permits; <10% success rate)

## TRAILHEAD & ACCESS LOGISTICS

### PRIMARY TRAILHEAD: SNOW LAKES
**Coordinates:** 47.5441°N, -120.7097°W  
**Elevation:** 1,300 feet  
**Directions:** From US-2 in Leavenworth, south on Icicle Creek Road (FR 76) for 4.2 miles  
**Parking:** Large paved lot; fills by 5–6 a.m. peak season  
**Facilities:** Outhouse only  
**Fee:** Northwest Forest Pass ($5 day / $30 annual)

**Trail Profile (Snow Lakes Trail #1553):**
- Distance to Snow Lakes: 7 miles one-way (14 miles round-trip)
- Elevation gain: 4,300 feet
- Difficulty: Hard (3.92/5 WTA rating)
- Character: 24 switchbacks through forest; notable downhill return with constant "jarring and pounding"
- Water refill: Snow Creek (trailhead) before switchback section
- **Critical:** Lower trail sections are dry; refill before descent
- **No dogs:** Dogs prohibited in Enchantment Permit Area

### ALTERNATIVE TRAILHEADS
**Stuart Lake Trailhead** (47.5278°N, -120.8205°W)
- Elevation: 3,400 ft
- Access: 8.5 mi Icicle Rd + 4 mi FR 7600/7601
- Parking: Severely limited; may require roadside parking
- Provides direct access to Colchuck Lake via Trail #1599A

**Eightmile Lake Trailhead** (FR 7601)
- Parking: Limited
- Road: Steep, washboarded; high-clearance vehicle recommended

### VEHICLE ACCESS & SEASONAL ROAD CLOSURE
- **Snow Lakes Trailhead Road (FR 76):** Maintained; typically open May–October
- **Forest Road 7601 (Stuart/Colchuck/Eightmile Access):** **Closed November–May**; reopens May 15
- **Winter Access:** Non-motorized use allowed (hiking, snowshoeing, cross-country skiing); avalanche danger significant

### NEAREST SERVICES: LEAVENWORTH
**Distance:** 4–15 miles from trailhead  
**Accommodations:** LOGE hotel (gear rental, café), Hampton Inn, multiple Bavarian-themed lodges  
**Supplies:** Safeway (main grocery), Mountain Modern Supply Co., Basecamp Outfitters, Performance Footwear  
**Other:** Wenatchee River Ranger District (USFS permits/conditions), shuttle services, breweries, dining

## PERMIT & ACCESS REGULATIONS

### OVERNIGHT PERMITS (May 15 – Oct 31)
**Cost:** $6 non-refundable application fee + $5/person/day camping (max 14-day stay)

**Allocation Breakdown:**
- **Advanced Lottery (75% of permits):** Feb 15–Mar 1 application window; <10% success rate; largest share
- **Daily Lottery:** Limited availability; applicants must be within 1-mile of Wenatchee River Ranger District
- **First-Come, First-Served:** Unclaimed/cancelled permits released via Recreation.gov; highly competitive

**Key Restrictions:**
- One permit per person per season (no overlapping permits)
- All group members must camp together (max group size typically 12)
- Camping only at previously impacted sites; no new impacts
- Fires prohibited above 5,000 ft or within 0.5 mi of lakes
- No standing trees may be cut

**Permit Zones:**
1. **Core Zone** (highest demand, most scenic)
2. **Colchuck Zone** (gateway to Core; established sites)
3. **Snow Zone** (near trailhead; less crowded)
4. **Stuart Zone** (alternative access)
5. **Eightmile/Caroline Zone** (northern access)

### DAY-USE PERMITS
- Free, self-issued at all trailheads
- No reservation needed

**Reservation Portal:** Recreation.gov
- Advanced Lottery: [recreation.gov/permits/233273](https://www.recreation.gov/permits/233273)
- Daily Lottery: [recreation.gov/permits/445863](https://www.recreation.gov/permits/445863)

## SEASONAL CONDITIONS & ACCESS WINDOWS

### APRIL–MAY (PRE-SEASON)
- **Before May 15:** No overnight permits required; exploit for lottery avoidance
- **Conditions:** Heavy maritime snow; firm mornings; afternoon rot; crampons essential
- **Hazards:** Avalanche risk; complex route-finding in snow; whiteout potential
- **Road Status:** FR 7601 still closed; Snow Lakes road may be accessible
- **Recommendation:** Advanced parties only; mountaineering equipment required

### JUNE (EARLY SEASON)
- **Conditions:** Early June—substantial snow; mid-June—variable; rapid warming
- **Aasgard Pass:** Often still snow-covered (typically melts by late June, but highly variable)
- **Road Status:** FR 7601 opens by June 15
- **Permits:** Begin June 15; lottery + daily options available
- **Hazards:** Lingering snow complicates route-finding; avalanche danger; rapid weather changes
- **Recommendation:** Flexible parties; expect variable conditions

### JULY–AUGUST (PEAK SEASON)
- **Conditions:** Minimal snow at moderate elevations; granite faces ideal and dry; cool nights; late-August residual ice
- **Temperature:** 62–63°F avg high; 42°F avg low; <1 inch monthly precipitation
- **Daylight:** 15–16 hours
- **Hazards:** Late-afternoon thunderstorms (frequent); unexpected snowfall even mid-summer; rockfall (late-summer loose terrain)
- **Access:** Peak permit demand; parking full by 5–6 a.m. weekends
- **Recommendation:** Premier rock climbing window; **arrive pre-dawn to avoid afternoon storms**

### SEPTEMBER (SHOULDER SEASON)
- **Conditions:** Excellent; residual snow on late slopes; crisp, stable weather
- **Temperature:** 57°F avg high; 38°F avg low
- **Crowds:** Moderate (lower than peak)
- **Recommendation:** Ideal for experienced climbers; excellent conditions; lower crowds

### OCTOBER (GOLDEN LARCH / EARLY WINTER)
- **Conditions:** Stunning scenery (early–mid Oct)—**golden larches, excellent visibility**
- **Temperature:** 48°F avg high; 32°F avg low; 6 inches monthly precipitation
- **Hazards:** Highly unpredictable weather; first snow events late month; rapidly shortening daylight
- **Road Status:** Permit season ends Oct 15
- **Recommendation:** Brief window of excellence; brief daylight; high scenic value; unpredictable weather

### NOVEMBER–MARCH (WINTER)
- **Conditions:** Heavy snowfall; established winter conditions by December; avalanche danger
- **Access:** FR 7601 closed; non-motorized use only; permit system ends
- **Hazards:** Avalanche (primary); extreme weather; route-finding impossible; very short daylight
- **Recommendation:** Expert parties with avalanche training only; typically requires guide services

- **Year-round:** Aasgard Pass typically snow-covered until late June; patches year-round

### 1. DRAGONTAIL PEAK (8,219 ft / 2,505 m)

**Location & Coordinates:** 47.4787°N, 120.8333°W  
**Prominence:** 1,760 feet  
**Region:** Stuart Range, North Cascades; highest in Enchantments

**From Trailhead:** 12–13.5 miles round-trip; 5,600–5,833 ft elevation gain; 9–14 hrs total

**Route Details:**

**SERPENTINE ARETE (Grade IV, 5.8, 13 pitches, ~2,000 ft)**
- **First Ascent:** Tom Hargis & Jay Ossiander (1973)
- **Character:** Aesthetic granite ridgeline; clean cracks + exposed slab traversing
- **Protection:** Medium trad rack (dbl 0.5–1.5"); 12+ draws; long runners
- **Crux:** Two linked 5.8 pitches with technical crack work
- **Time:** 10–12 hrs climbing only
- **Season:** Year-round (snow/ice complicate early season)

**BACKBONE RIDGE / FIN DIRECT (Grade IV+, 5.9, 12 pitches, ~2,000 ft)**
- **First Ascent:** Backbone—Bonneville & Weigelt (1970); Fin Direct—Cruver & Lewis (1975)
- **Character:** Clean granite + scrambling; standout fin formation
- **Protection:** #6 Camalot for offwidth; medium to #3.5; #5 cam useful
- **Time:** 12+ hrs climbing only
- **Season:** Typically 2-day bivouac
- **Notes:** More sustained than Serpentine; solid 5.9 ability required

**DRAGONS OF EDEN (Grade V, 5.12a, 7 pitches, 650 ft)**
- **First Ascent:** Wallace & McGown (1988, aid); FFA—Jens Holsten (2008)
- **Character:** Vertical granite panel (granodiorite); three 5.11 pitches + one 5.12 crux
- **Protection:** Trad climbing
- **Time:** 8–10 hrs on rock
- **Often continued with NE Buttress (Grade III, 4th–5.7, 1,600 additional ft)**
- **Notes:** Expert-level free climbing required; awesome crack climbing

**THIS, MY FRIEND (Grade III, 5.10a, 5 pitches, 900 ft)**
- **First Ascent:** Andy Wyatt & Chris Potts (Aug 2020, onsight/ground-up)
- **Character:** Quality sustained crack climbing + face work; East Face; varied styles
- **Protection:** Dbl 0.2–#2, single 0.1 & #3; stoppers & runners
- **Time:** 6–8 hours
- **Notes:** Modern route; onsight-friendly; active community development

**TRIPLE COULOIRS (Grade IV, WI3 M3, ~1,600 ft)**
- **Type:** Mixed alpine climbing—snow, ice, and rock
- **Best Season:** December–May (typically guide service only)
- **Time:** One long day or 2-day alpine expedition
- **Notes:** Steep snow/ice with rock sections; alpine experience required

### 2. AASGARD PASS (7,841 ft / 2,390 m)

**Route Type:** Alpine scramble/mountaineering pass  
**Difficulty:** Class 3 (Hard alpine scramble)  
**Distance from Colchuck Lake:** 0.8 mi; 2,200 ft elevation gain  
**Time:** 1–1.5 hrs ascent; 1–2 hrs descent  
**Critical Bottleneck:** Gateway to Core Zone; most dangerous and navigationally confusing section

**Route Description:**
- From Colchuck Lake, rock-hop around lake's head, then transition to "staircase-like" grade trail
- Final push: Nearly 2,000 ft gain in ~0.75 mi over boulder fields
- "Thigh-burning, chest-bursting endeavor"
- Often has false summit; route-finding experience essential

**Key Features:**
- Scree terrain "near vertical at times"
- Exposed scrambling sections
- Hidden crevasses and avalanche terrain
- Streams melting from beneath creating unstable snow conditions (year-round)
- **Descent Navigation Critical:** Common error = veering west; trend climber's-left; follow cairns

**Snow Conditions:**
- Often snow-covered most of year
- Typically melts by late June (highly variable; often extends into July+)
- Early season: Requires crampons, ice axes, mountaineering expertise
- Crevasses and avalanche danger present

**Hazards:**
- Loose rock and exposed scrambling
- Route-finding errors (especially descent)
- Fatigue-related errors on steep terrain
- Snow coverage (pre–July)
- Avalanche terrain
- **Notable:** Injuries, search and rescue missions, and fatalities have occurred

**Seasonal Access:**
- **Before Late June:** Crampons, ice axes, and mountaineering equipment required
- **Early Departure Recommended:** Avoid midday heat during snow travel
- **Summer:** Suitable for Class 3 rock scrambling with good fitness

### 4. McCLELLAN PEAK (8,363 ft / 2,550 m) — Bulger Peak

**Route Type:** Alpine scramble  
**Difficulty:** Up to Class 3 scramble  
**Prominence:** Bulger peak status (prominence >500 ft)

**Route Description:**
- Approach: Via Colchuck Lake, traverse around to Aasgard Pass, work toward Perfection Lake
- Final ascent: Short Class 3 scramble to ridge
- Summit traverse: Small ridge walk and low-class scrambling
- **Notable:** Summit "fits only two people kind of tiny"
- **Psychological:** Exposure on traverse; minimal actual fall risk

**Base Camp Strategy:**
- Position base camp above Inspiration Lake for day ascents
- Centrally located for accessing multiple Bulgers in single trip

**Multi-Peak Strategy:** Can be summited with Little Annapurna in one day
- Combined distance: ~22 miles
- Combined elevation gain: ~7,000 feet
- Best for acclimatized parties with early start

**Time Estimate:** 6–8 hours round-trip from base camp

## IDEAL MULTI-PEAK BASECAMP STRATEGY

**Recommended Base Camp Location:** Just above Inspiration Lake or near Crystal/Perfection saddle in Core Zone

**Permit Requirement:** Core Zone overnight permit (highest demand; <10% lottery success rate)

**Multi-Day Expedition Strategy:**
- **Day 1:** Approach and acclimatize
- **Day 2:** Little Annapurna (easy warm-up) + possibly McClellan Peak
- **Day 3:** Enchantment Peak or additional Bulger ascents
- **Day 4:** Dragontail Peak technical climbing or Class 3/4 scrambling
- **Day 5:** Descent and exit

**Trip Duration:** 3+ days minimum for acclimatization and multiple objectives

**Early-Season Opportunity (April–May 15):**
- Overnight permits NOT required before May 15
- Access via Snow Lakes or Stuart Lake trails without reservation
- Spring expedition possible with mountaineering equipment (crampons, ice axes, avalanche gear)
- Snowshoes may be necessary

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR TRIP PLANNING

1. **Permits are Essential:** Overnight permits (May 15–Oct 31) highly competitive; apply Feb 15–Mar 1 for best chances. Day-use permits free and self-issued.

2. **Aasgard Pass is Critical & Dangerous:** Not maintained; requires scrambling experience and excellent route-finding. Snow coverage varies; spring attempts require mountaineering gear and expertise.

3. **Peak Season = July–August:** Best weather and accessibility; highest crowds; warmest temps; driest conditions; afternoon thunderstorms common. **Arrive pre-dawn to avoid electrical risk.**

4. **Shoulder Seasons Offer Value:** June and September/early October provide fewer crowds, still-good conditions, and lower permit pressure (use daily lottery or visit pre-May 15).

5. **Road Closure Matters:** FR 7601 closes Nov–May; only Snow Lakes Trailhead reliably accessible earlier in season.

6. **Water Abundant in Upper Basin:** Fill containers before descending from Snow Lakes; lower trail sections dry.

7. **Mosquitoes Peak July–August:** Bring head nets and repellent; severe in peak season; less issue in shoulder seasons.

8. **Basecamp Above Inspiration Lake Optimal:** Central location for day ascents to multiple Bulger peaks and accessing Core Zone objectives.

9. **Nearby Services Excellent:** Leavenworth has full pre/post-trip infrastructure (groceries, gear rental, lodging, dining).

10. **Multi-Peak Climbing Significant:** Area offers exceptional mountaineering and alpine rock climbing; Dragontail's technical routes (Serpentine Arete, Backbone Ridge, Dragons of Eden) are high-quality granite climbing.
