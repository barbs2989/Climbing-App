Hazards & Objective Dangers

Primary Hazards
  • Avalanche: Wet, heavy maritime snow; avalanche danger highest March–June; exposed snow chutes above LaCrosse Basin HIGH
  • Rockfall: Loose, fragmented basalt; summit blocks and scree fields; unstable talus HIGH
  • Crevasses: Hanging glaciers south of Anderson Pass; adjacent Anderson & Eel glaciers have active crevasses (though Anderson Glacier mostly moribund) HIGH
  • Weather: Rapid weather deterioration; hurricane-force winds; marine west coast climate delivers rain/snow from Pacific; high annual precipitation HIGH
  • Navigation: Snow obscures trail at all elevations early/late season; map & compass navigation essential HIGH
  • Exposure & Terrain: Steep, exposed alpine terrain; potential for long falls on loose rock MEDIUM
Glacier & Crevasse Context
Anderson Glacier Status
Largely extinct; no significant crevasses; mostly boulder field and terminal moraine lake; has shrunk dramatically HIGH
Eel Glacier Status
Still active (opposite Anderson); late-summer bergschrund difficult to cross; large crevasses on eastern slopes HIGH
Mount LaCrosse Hanging Glaciers
Cliffs and hanging glaciers exist south of Anderson Pass on Mount LaCrosse; potential ice/rock hazard from above MEDIUM
