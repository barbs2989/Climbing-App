## Money-Saving Tips

1. **Annual Passes:**
   - Northwest Forest Pass: $30/year (covers USFS trailheads)
   - America the Beautiful: $80/year (covers ALL federal lands)
   - Mount Rainier Annual: $55/year (just Rainier NP)

2. **Multi-Peak Trips:**
   - Single NW Forest Pass ($5) covers multiple USFS peaks in one trip
   - Buy annual if doing 6+ USFS peaks per year

3. **Mount Rainier Tip:**
   - Walk-up permits ~33% available at ranger stations (same day or day before)
   - Advance reservation for 2/3 of slots May-September

4. **Mount Adams/Baker/Shuksan:**
   - Free wilderness permits (self-issue)
   - Only pay parking pass ($5/day or $30/year pass)

## Most Popular vs. Most Remote

**Busiest Peaks** (Plan early, expect crowds):
1. Mount Rainier - Most regulated, permits fill fast
2. Liberty Bell Group - Popular rock climbing destination
3. Mount Stuart/Dragontail - Lottery permits (popular when available)
4. Mount Baker - Well-known glacier climbing
5. Ruby Mountain - Easy day hike, parking fills weekends

**Most Remote** (Multi-day approaches, few visitors):
1. Glacier Peak - 12+ miles approach, storm-damaged access
2. Indian Head Peak - Via Pacific Crest Trail, seldom visited
3. Mount Lago - Remote Pasayten summit
4. Robinson Mountain - Remote Pasayten approach
5. Remmel Mountain - Remote cathedral range

## Quick Trip Checklist

Before any climb, ensure you have:

- [ ] Required permit(s) obtained
- [ ] Parking pass ($5 cash or pre-purchased annual)
- [ ] Weather forecast checked (NWAC if avalanche terrain)
- [ ] Road status confirmed (WSDOT, NPS website)
- [ ] Physical condition adequate for route
- [ ] Crevasse rescue equipment if needed (rope, harness, protection)
- [ ] Group size within limits
- [ ] Emergency communication device (satellite messenger)
