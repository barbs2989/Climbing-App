## Routes by Peak

### MOUNT STUART (9,415 ft)
Mount Stuart is in the Alpine Lakes Wilderness near Leavenworth, WA. It's built on the Late Cretaceous Mount Stuart batholith—a massive granitic pluton with clean, sound granite noted for extensive jointing.

#### 1. **West Ridge**
- **YDS Grade**: 5.4
- **Alpine Grade**: PD+
- **Elevation**: 9,415 ft
- **Elevation Gain**: 5,175 ft
- **Pitches**: 10
- **Route Length**: 701m
- **Climb Type**: Alpine rock
- **Best Season**: Jun-Sep
- **Description**: The easiest technical route up Mount Stuart, a long ridge of mostly 3rd and 4th class with a couple of low-5th steps, first climbed in August 1935.
- **Primary Hazards**: Route-finding on a long ridge with several towers and notches; loose rock in places; exposure on the crest
- **Required Gear**: Light alpine rack, nuts, small-medium cams, 60m rope

#### 2. **North Face**
- **YDS Grade**: 5.8
- **Alpine Grade**: D
- **Elevation**: 9,415 ft
- **Elevation Gain**: 6,015 ft
- **Climb Type**: Alpine mixed
- **Best Season**: Apr-Aug
- **Description**: A large mixed alpine face on Mount Stuart's north side, combining glacier travel with steep snow, ice, and rock between the Stuart Glacier Couloir and the Ice Cliff Glacier.
- **Primary Hazards**: Icefall and rockfall off surrounding rock bands (worsened by glacier recession); crevasses on approach glacier (especially later season)
- **Required Gear**: Ice axe, crampons, ice screws, alpine rock rack

#### 3. **The Gendarme**
- **YDS Grade**: 5.8-5.9+
- **Alpine Grade**: D
- **Elevation**: 9,415 ft
- **Elevation Gain**: 5,147 ft
- **Pitches**: 2
- **Climb Type**: Alpine rock
- **Best Season**: Jul-Sep
- **Description**: The famous crux tower on Mount Stuart's North Ridge, a pinnacle first climbed directly by James Wickwire and Fred Stanley in 1964. Offers two pitches of the most serious climbing on the ridge.
- **Primary Hazards**: Strenuous off-width and lieback crux; high exposure on the ridge crest
- **Required Gear**: Standard rack to #4 (a #3 and #4 cam especially useful for the offwidth pitch)

#### 4. **North Ridge (Complete)**
- **YDS Grade**: 5.9
- **Alpine Grade**: D
- **Elevation**: 9,415 ft
- **Elevation Gain**: 6,015 ft
- **Pitches**: 18
- **Route Length**: 366m
- **Climb Type**: Alpine rock
- **Best Season**: Jul-Sep
- **Description**: One of the most celebrated alpine rock climbs in the Cascades and one of the original Fifty Classic Climbs of North America, capped by the dramatic Gendarme crux.
- **Primary Hazards**: Exposed, sometimes loose rock along the ridge crest; afternoon thunderstorms are a real risk on this long, exposed route
- **Required Gear**: Single rack to 3", nuts, alpine draws, 60m rope

#### 5. **Girth Pillar**
- **YDS Grade**: 5.11-
- **Alpine Grade**: TD (Very Difficult)
- **Elevation**: 9,415 ft
- **Elevation Gain**: 6,016 ft
- **Pitches**: 11
- **Route Length**: 610m
- **Climb Type**: Alpine rock
- **Best Season**: Jul-Aug
- **Description**: A steep, clean rock pillar rising out of the Ice Cliff Glacier on Mount Stuart's northeast face, first climbed by Kit Lewis and Jim Nelson in 1983. Offers sustained modern rock climbing at elevation.
- **Primary Hazards**: Large loose blocks reported on P3 (5.10+) pitch—test holds carefully; icefall/crevasse hazard from Ice Cliff Glacier approach beneath the pillar
- **Required Gear**: Alpine rock rack, cams to 3in, nuts, ice axe

### PRUSIK PEAK (8,008 ft) – Enchantments Region
Prusik Peak is an iconic granite spire above Gnome Tarn in the Enchantments, renowned for Grade III rock climbs on immaculate granite. All Prusik Peak routes require the Enchantments approach via Stuart Lake/Colchuck Lake trail (~8-10 mi roundtrip).

#### 6. **West Ridge**
- **YDS Grade**: 5.7
- **Alpine Grade**: AD (Moderately Difficult)
- **Elevation**: 8,008 ft
- **Elevation Gain**: 4,608 ft
- **Pitches**: 4
- **Route Length**: 122m
- **Climb Type**: Alpine rock
- **Best Season**: May-Oct
- **Description**: One of the most popular moderate alpine rock climbs in Washington. Four pitches of clean, featured granite lead along an airy crest to the small summit, with climbing rarely exceeding 5.7 yet feeling thoroughly alpine in exposure and position.
- **Primary Hazards**: Afternoon thunderstorms with limited retreat high on the ridge; rockfall from parties climbing above on this popular line; early-season snow/ice on approach ledges
- **Required Gear**: Rack to 3.5in (small cams/nuts through #4-equivalent), 60m rope, ice axe and crampons (early-season), helmet

#### 7. **South Face (Burgner-Stanley)**
- **YDS Grade**: 5.10a
- **Alpine Grade**: D
- **Elevation**: 8,008 ft
- **Elevation Gain**: 4,608 ft
- **Pitches**: 6
- **Route Length**: 152m
- **Climb Type**: Alpine rock
- **Best Season**: May-Oct
- **Description**: The classic South Face of Prusik Peak—one of the most celebrated alpine rock routes in the Enchantments, put up in 1968 by Ron Burgner and Fred Stanley. Steep, well-protected crack and face climbing on superb granite.
- **Primary Hazards**: Sustained crack climbing; exposure; loose rock on some pitches; long Enchantments approach
- **Required Gear**: Alpine rock rack, cams to 3in, nuts, 60m rope

#### 8. **Solid Gold**
- **YDS Grade**: 5.11a
- **Alpine Grade**: D
- **Elevation**: 8,008 ft
- **Elevation Gain**: 4,608 ft
- **Pitches**: 5
- **Route Length**: 137m
- **Climb Type**: Rock
- **Best Season**: May-Oct
- **Description**: Climbs the steep south face of Prusik Peak on immaculate golden granite. Offers several pitches of sustained, well-protected crack and face climbing at a stiffer grade than the popular west ridge. A coveted free climb for strong parties.
- **Primary Hazards**: Sustained 5.11 climbing; serious exposure on the steep face; afternoon thunderstorms; rope management on steep rappels
- **Required Gear**: Double rack including cams to ~3in for corner/crack pitches, 60m rope for descent rappel

#### 9. **Der Sportsman**
- **YDS Grade**: 5.11
- **Alpine Grade**: D
- **Elevation**: 8,008 ft
- **Elevation Gain**: 4,608 ft
- **Pitches**: 6
- **Route Length**: 183m
- **Climb Type**: Alpine rock
- **Best Season**: May-Oct
- **Description**: A harder free climb on Prusik Peak's south face, climbing sustained, technical crack lines on the same superb Enchantments granite as the classic Burgner-Stanley. A coveted test-piece for strong trad climbers in a spectacular alpine setting.
- **Primary Hazards**: Sustained hard crack climbing; exposure; afternoon thunderstorms; long Enchantments approach
- **Required Gear**: Double rack weighted toward smaller cams/nuts, 60m rope

### DRAGONTAIL PEAK (8,840 ft) – Enchantments Region

#### 10. **Gerber-Sink (North Face Route)**
- **YDS Grade**: 5.7
- **Alpine Grade**: IV
- **Elevation**: 8,840 ft
- **Elevation Gain**: 5,440 ft
- **Pitches**: 10
- **Route Length**: 610m
- **Climb Type**: Alpine rock/mixed
- **Best Season**: Jul-Sep (rock) or Feb-Apr (ice/mixed), conditions-dependent
- **Description**: Also known as the North Face route, Gerber-Sink climbs the lowest point of Dragontail's north face (nordwand), between Triple Couloirs and Backbone Ridge, above Colchuck Lake. Originally a 5.7 summer rock route; in recent years has become a popular winter/spring ice-and-mixed objective.
- **Primary Hazards**: Rockfall; loose rock; lingering snow at base; thin/variable ice and mixed climbing in winter; weather exposure on big face
- **Required Gear**: Medium rack to 3 inches (summer); 4+ ice screws including stubby (winter); single rock rack and pitons (winter); two ice tools and crampons (winter); helmet; 60m rope

## Approach Information

### **Enchantments Area (Prusik Peak, Dragontail Peak, Colchuck Peak)**
- **Trailhead**: Stuart Lake Trailhead (3,400 ft)
- **Total Distance**: ~8-10 miles roundtrip to Colchuck Lake
- **Key Waypoints**:
  - Colchuck Lake (5,570 ft) – standard base camp for all north-side routes
  - Aasgard Pass approach – steep, loose scree/talus (~2,000 ft gain in < 1 mile)
- **Permits**: Enchantment Permit Area (quota limit); day-use wilderness permit required
- **Overnight Policy**: May 15-Oct 31 overnight permit season
- **Notes**: Dogs prohibited; cell coverage unreliable; satellite communicator recommended

### **Mount Stuart Approach**
- **Trailhead**: Esmeralda Trailhead (4,290 ft, end of FR-9737 in North Fork Teanaway)
- **Via**: Longs Pass (6,210 ft) or Ingalls Pass (~6,500 ft)
- **Permit**: Northwest Forest Pass required

## Notable Features

### Prusik Peak – Pure Rock Climbing Focus
The Enchantments routes on Prusik Peak represent some of Washington's finest alpine rock climbing:
- **Solid Gold** (5.11a): Modern free climbing on perfect granite with serious exposure
- **South Face/Burgner-Stanley** (5.10a): Classic route established 1968, iconic Enchantments tick
- **Der Sportsman** (5.11): Test-piece for strong crack climbers; sustained technical climbing
- **West Ridge** (5.7): Most popular; 4 pitches of moderate climbing with excellent position

### Mount Stuart – Long Alpine Rock Routes
Mount Stuart routes are longer, more committing:
- **North Ridge Complete** (5.9, 18 pitches): One of the Fifty Classic Climbs of North America
- **Girth Pillar** (5.11-, 11 pitches): Modern, sustained climbing at 9,000+ ft elevation
- **West Ridge** (5.4, 10 pitches): Longest route; mostly class 3-4 with sustained exposure

### Seasonal Windows
- **Summer (Jun-Sep)**: All routes accessible via dry approach
- **Spring (Apr-May)**: Glacier approaches available; some high-elevation routes may have snow
- **Winter (Dec-Mar)**: Ice/mixed variants available on Dragontail; most rock routes challenging due to approach snow
