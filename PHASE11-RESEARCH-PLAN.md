# Phase 11: International Alpine Routes Research

**Status**: Launching  
**Date**: 2026-07-16  
**Scope**: European Alps + major international peaks  
**Target**: 40-60 international climbing routes, 150+ hazard entries

## Research Agents (5 parallel)

### Agent 1: Mont Blanc Massif Routes
- Mont Blanc (Goûter Route, Three Montenvers, Bosses Ridge)
- Mont Blanc du Tacul
- Mont Maudit
- Aiguille du Midi climbing variants
- **Focus**: Crowding, crevasses, objective hazards, seasonal windows

### Agent 2: Gran Paradiso & Alps South
- Gran Paradiso (Normal Route + variants)
- Monte Rosa (Parrotspitze Normal)
- Breithorn (all approaches)
- **Focus**: Italian Alpine access, permits, scramble vs climbing distinction

### Agent 3: Ecrins & Chamonix Valley Crags
- Barre des Écrins
- Aiguille de la Tour
- Chamonix sport climbing crags
- **Focus**: French permit system, via ferrata vs climbing, guide requirements

### Agent 4: Elbrus & Caucasus
- Mount Elbrus (South/North sides)
- Kazbegi peaks
- Elbrus commercial guide services
- **Focus**: Commercial climbing, permits, altitude hazards, weather windows

### Agent 5: Miscellaneous Classics
- Jungfrau/Eiger variants
- Zugspitze climbing routes
- Matterhorn (various routes, not just hiking)
- **Focus**: Well-known objectives, grade accuracy, hazard consensus

## Hazard Focus
- Altitude/AMS hazards (Mont Blanc 4,808m+)
- Crowding & bottlenecks
- Objective hazards (seracs, rockfall, crevasses)
- Permit requirements per country
- Commercial guide market
- Grade discrepancies (Alpine grade system)

## Sources
- Mountain Project International
- CampToBlog (Chamonix/Alps)
- Summit Post European routes
- Alpine Club guidebooks
- Recent trip reports (2024-2026)
- Commercial guide operators (Chamonix Guides, Mountain Wilderness)

## Success Criteria
- 40-60 verified routes
- 150+ hazard entries
- Multi-source verification (2-5+ sources per route)
- Current permit/access info
- Grade accuracy confirmed
